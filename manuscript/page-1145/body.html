"Meg" on the first lid, smooth and fair.I look in with loving eyes,For folded here, with well-known care,A goodly gathering lies,The record of a peaceful life—Gifts to gentle child and girl,A bridal gown, lines to a wife,A tiny shoe, a baby curl.No toys in this first chest remain,For all are carried away,In their old age, to join againIn another small Meg's play.Ah, happy mother! Well I knowYou hear, like a sweet refrain,Lullabies ever soft and lowIn the falling summer rain.
